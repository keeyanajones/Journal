RESULTS

  1. Reach: Post reach is the number of unique users who saw your post. How far is your content spreading across social? Is it actually reaching user’s feeds? In the face of ever-changing organic algorithms, tracking reach is arguably more important than ever.
  2. Clicks: This is the number of clicks on your content, company name or logo. Link clicks are critical toward understanding how users move through your marketing funnel. Tracking clicks per campaign is essential to understand what drives curiosity or encourages people to buy.
  3. Engagement: The total number of social interactions divided by the number of impressions. For engagement, it’s about seeing who interacted and if it was a good ratio out of your total reach. This sheds light on how well your audience perceives you and their willingness to interact.
  4. Hashtag performance: What were your most used hashtags on your own side? Which hashtags were most associated with your brand? Or what hashtags created the most engagement?
  5. Organic and paid likes: More than just standard Likes, these likes are defined from paid or organic content. For channels like Facebook, organic engagement is much harder to gain traction, which is why many brands turn to Facebook Ads. However, earning organic likes on Instagram isn’t quite as difficult.
  6. Sentiment: This is the measurement of how users reacted to your content, brand or hashtag. Did customers find your recent campaign offensive? What type of sentiment are people associating with your campaign hashtag? It’s always better to dig deeper and find what people are saying.

DISCUSSION

Open ended questions help you gather information, qualify sales opportunities, and establish rapport, trust, and credibility with potential customers.

Information gathering

  • What prompted you/ your company to look into this?
  • What are your expectations/ requirements for this product/ service?
  • What process did you go through to determine your needs?
  • How do you see this happening?
  • What is it that you’d like to see accomplished?
  • With whom have you had success in the past?
  • With whom have you had difficulties in the past?
  • Can you help me understand that a little better?
  • What does that mean?
  • How does that process work now?
  • What challenges does that process create?
  • What challenges has that created in the past?
  • What are the best things about that process?
  • What other items should we discuss?

    • Qualifying

  • What do you see as the next action steps?
  • What is your timeline for implementing/ purchasing this type of service/ product?
  • What other data points should we know before moving forward?
  • What budget has been established for this?
  • What are your thoughts?
  • Who else is involved in this decision?
  • What could make this no longer a priority?
  • What’s changed since we last talked?
  • What concerns do you have?

    • Establishing rapport, trust & credibility

  • How did you get involved in…?
  • What kind of challenges are you facing?
  • What’s the most important priority to you with this? Why?
  • What other issues are important to you?
  • What would you like to see improved?
  • How do you measure that?

CONCLUSIONS

A good consistent social media strategy will:

  • get more traffic
  • relationship building
  • better customer satisfaction/retention
  • better customer service

The foundation of the strategy is to understand who your target audience is and why they use social media. These demographics and psychographics refer to statistics that describe a group of people. Not all of these will be relevant to your business.

Demographics

Determining demographics depends on whether or not you already have a site and traffic:

  • If you don't have a site or traffic yet, find a similar site and use it for analysis.
  • If you have a site that does have traffic, you have a few options.

First, you can use typical search engine tools to find your competitors. Go to the competitors web sites and use their search features. The more popular the site is, the more accurate the data will be.

Another option is to use the keyword searches that describes your niche. For example, in my case I would enter “live node.js game development”. Try several keywords to get a good idea of your target market.

A final way to get demographic information is with Analytics tools offered by the platform or any analytic tool of choice. If you haven’t enabled these before, you might have to do so and wait 24 hours to get data. However, once data is collected, you will see graphs for relevant data that represents your actual audience.

In most cases your platform’s analytics is the best source of information, if available. Try to record all this data in a text or spreadsheet files. So far, my demographics for the new site:

  • Gender: largely males with very few females.
  • Education: relatively educated, but slightly more than Internet average.
  • Location: most likely from US, UK, and India.
  • Age: average age is about 30.
  • Income: likely medium to medium high income due to relatively career and age.

Notice that some of those parameters are more definite than others. The first time, estimate some demographics. Over time, refine these as more is learned about the actual audience.

Psychographics

Psychographics tell us the why create content and products that the target audience actually wants, to understand more about the audience’s outlook on topic. Questions to answer include:

  • Why do they want to learn about [the niche]?
  • How important is [the niche] to them? (i.e., is it a hobby or part of their job?)
  • How do they like to learn? (e.g., video, text, audio, etc., and on what type of device)
  • What common questions do they have about [the niche]?
    • How knowledgeable are they about [the niche]?

This is crucial strategically because there are no tools that give URL or keyword search results with psychographic information. The only way to answer these questions is to observe the target audience. The idea is to find your exact target audience.

For example, If you’re targeting those who are interested in javaScript development based on the latest libraries and frameworks, that group of people is going to be different from the group interested in web development because of node application development.

    Next, simply watch and read the descriptions or introductions. If you’re not familiar with the platform, read the guide for that particular platform to understand what you’re looking at first. You will see alot of the same type of information over and over again. The information will tell you what people are interested in learning about, while the most repeated data will help you understand how people like to learn about it.

    Typically, you can filter result based on some criteria; for example, most popular of all time. Some of these results will be dominated by videos from the same content creator. In this case, that alone tells how this community likes to consume information. After looking at the first 100 links or so, you see that this community cares about:

    • rest api
    • security
    • all in one video
    • learning about express, mongodb, and angular
    • creating practical clean code with JavaScript

    2 - Forums:
    Another place where you can see your target readers interact is in a forum. Just search Google for “[niche] forum,” and you’ll get a list of results. In some cases, there will be a specific forum for you niche. Stack Overflow is one of the most popular forums for technology and has the ability to be linked to other

    NOTE: Careful forums that have the readers you are interested in attracting. Otherwise, your results will be inaccurate. There’s a big difference between someone learning about javascript to create a api and someone looking to create a game.

    3 - Comments:
    If you can find a popular blog and livestream discussions that relates very closely to the niche, this may be an option. For example, the comments on a Youtube channel discussion question. Read through the comments and record observations about the readers.
